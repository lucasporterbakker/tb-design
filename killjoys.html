Killjoys

Home / Killjoys



Description

Costuming for the future requires innovation - all the costuming for the series is conceptualized then built or adapted to bring a unique and signature look to life. To create the look for a galaxy unlike our own, I find inspiration in avant-garde pieces from several time periods as well as contemporary designers such as Alexander McQueen, Versace, and Mugler.

Each planet in the series’ system has its own distinct aesthetic, from colour palette and design to fabrics. Greys, blues, blacks, and muddy browns influenced by smoke, smog, and pollution are common to ‘Westerley’, the planet of factories. Breakdown is an essential element of this evolved look.

‘Leith’ is the agrarian planet. It’s home to a giant bazaar filled with peoples from all galaxies peddling their wares, buying produce, drugs, and clothes. This design is modelled after a Moroccan Souk with vibrant colours, patterns and textures.

‘Q’resh, the water planet, is the manor borne, with wealth to buy the finest fabrics, rare metals, and precious stones. I used Sapphire, Ruby, Diamond, Jade, and Topaz as colour inspiration.
