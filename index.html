Trysha Bakker has been designing for film and television for over 40 years. After graduating in Fashion Design, her love of film led her to imagine a confluence of these two passions, and ultimately to pursue a career in costume design. As a fan of historical fiction, Period Drama became a space she thrived in. Evidenced by credits such as: Maudie (set in 1930-1960), starring Sally Hawkins and Ethan Hawke; the mini-series Shattered City (1917); and the 3 American Girl films (1904, 1934, and 1944). She also loves to design at the forefront of contemporary film, with credits like: Netflix’s series FUBAR, with Arnold Schwarzenegger; the HBO series Run, with Merritt Weaver, Domhnall Gleeson, and Phoebe Waller Bridge; Bravo's Imposters; and USA's Covert Affairs. And the challenges of designing for fantasy and futuristic works such as Disney’s Zombies Trilogy; and the SyFy series Killjoys, has been a space she describes as a ‘creative playground.’ Trysha is comfortable designing for all genres, leading innovative design teams through the past, present, and future.
